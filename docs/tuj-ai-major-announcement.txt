Temple University Japan Advances Academic Offerings with Launch of Bachelor of Science in Artificial Intelligence for Fall 2026

Temple University, Japan Campus (TUJ) will introduce a new undergraduate major, a Bachelor of Science in Artificial Intelligence, beginning in the Fall 2026 semester. The new major responds to increasing demand from both students and corporate employers in Japan and across the Asia-Pacific region.

The degree is drawn from Temple University's College of Science and Technology curriculum and provides foundational training in programming, machine learning, data science, and AI applications. The program also emphasizes ethics, human-AI interaction, and hands-on project work, combining technical rigor with applied learning.

The program is delivered entirely in English in Japan, offering a distinctive pathway in Asia from a top global university at an affordable price compared to other top-tier global universities. Students may also complete portions of their degree at Temple's main campus in the United States while taking advantage of TUJ's comparatively affordable tuition.

President and Dean Matthew J. Wilson stated that artificial intelligence is reshaping industries and redefining the skills students need to succeed in a global economy.

TUJ has operated for 44 years as Japan's oldest and largest foreign university branch campus, and enrolled over 3,500 degree-seeking students by Fall 2025 — a near-tripling of undergraduate enrollment over five years. The university is expanding facilities, including the Hillside Center, an 8-story, 7,000-square-meter building in Kawasaki, opening August 2026. The Bachelor of Science in Artificial Intelligence will also be available at TUJ's Kyoto location.

Related developments: TUJ launched a full four-year Computer Science degree in 2024, an Honors Program in 2025, and certificates in esports and English language teaching.
